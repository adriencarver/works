Novels/Novellas

Gloryland

In the long ago summer of 2010, Evan Barker’s life is shit. His older brother, Jason, has taken his own life, leaving behind a family shattered by grief. His parents are buckling under the weight of the Great Recession, his so-called friends are aimless, drunken douchebags, and his younger sister has been institutionalized after discovering Jason’s body.

In a desperate leap for meaning and change, Evan decides to audition for American Idol in Nashville. Never mind that he hasn’t ever sung a note in his life.

Joining him on this reckless, possibly absurd trip is Lily Trent, a childhood acquaintance turned unexpected companion. A newly minted dancer at Deja Blue, Lily is just as lost as Evan, harboring her own secret demons and motivations. She’s also more than aware of the massive crush the hapless, virginal Evan has developed on her.

What starts as a desperate bid for stardom and relevance morphs into something else-- a search for meaning, connection, and self-worth in a generation raised on shoddy promises.

Johnny assumes collecting the souls of the damned will free him from the highways of purgatory, but salvation isn't the only thing waiting for him at the end of the road.

Emo

It's 2088 and Elizabeth London is turning 100. Time to go to the Epicity to get retired aka medically euthanized via nitrogen asphyxiation. She’s aided by her Emotionally-Minded Omnibot, an AI hospice nurse. On the way down, she listens to an old mix CD-- a parting gift from her first boyfriend in the fall of 2006.

The songs activate memories long forgotten, and Elizabeth relives moments from her first week at college as she makes her final journey through the fantastical dystopian underground humanity has built for itself in the wake of catastrophic climate change.
